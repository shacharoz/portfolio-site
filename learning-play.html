How innovative pedagogy, playfulness and meaningful learning intersect

Learning Cycle by Liron Levi & Shachar Oz, 2014

Curiosity is the key for meaningful learning. We all agree on that. But how do you generate curiosity in students that do not always want to be engaged? How do you continuously feed it over time?

In short, we believe that by offering play you might be able to create meaningful experience for the user which would end with moments of enlightenment. This should trigger more curiosity and develop passion. This talk tries to explain how good video games are built to create that process.


Oz, S., Levi L. (2015). How innovative pedagogy, playfulness and meaningful learning intersect. Akre: Teach First Israel Youth Center.

Oz, S. (2014). The science behind fun. In Perceptual computing learning forum. Jerusalem: Intel offices.

Levi L., Oz, S. (2014). How innovative pedagogy, playfulness and meaningful learning intersect. Qatzrin: Ohalo College for education, science and sports.

Predicting player personality through play behavior

Can the player behavior inside the game predict what the best job is for them? I claim that the way you play a game and the choices you make, can say a lot about your personality. This can be used to create a diagnostic tool for HR systems at all ages.


Oz, S. (2014). Predicting player personality through play behavior. In Y. Yair, & E. Shmueli (eds.), New technologies and their evaluation in online teaching and learning (pp. 313-317). Tel Aviv: Lewinsky College of education.

101 for video games

Why people love to play so much? Why players get so involved? Why do they play more than they watch TV? What happens inside the game that leaves the player with a feeling of "I want more"?

Suited lecture for those who never played video games before. I explained what is a game and how it is different from a toy and a movie. Talked about the different genres and each of their fun factors. Taught some basic concept in game design, and finally about how game design and learning connects.


Oz, S. (2013). Dissecting the game: video game genres, game mechanics and the connection between games and education. Tel Aviv: Center of educational technology (MindCET).

SIMILARITIES BETWEEN GAME DESIGN AND INSTRUCTIONAL DESIGN

Educational and instructional design could learn from good game design at how to tangle the users into the web of curiosity, challenge and fun. I show how these two design fields are actually quite similar.

I have identified 9 design principals in the game design theory that can be used to make a great instructional design. Let the user feel as the messiah (without them the game will not progress); setup the right rules system for the activity and allow users to play within it; let your users feel as if they are in control of the system; give feedback as fast as you can, as precise as possible; setup both grand goals and small achievements; balance the amount of content; balance the challenge and the ability of the player; build the feeling of success; and assist your users to reflect on their performance.


Oz, S. (2014). Similarities between instructional design and game design. In G. Ravid, & E. Shmueli (eds.), Games & gamification. Tel Aviv: Open University.

Oz, S. (2013). Similarities between instructional design and game design. HIT learning technologies department graduates conference. Holon: HIT.

USING AUGMENTED REALITY AND GAMES IN THE CLASSROOM

Presenting Augmented Reality and video games to the students at the Information and Communication Technology, an apprenticeship aimed to improve the tech-accessibility skills for teachers and educators at Mofet institute.

Tried to explain what is so wonderful in these two technologies and how they can be incorporated into the classroom. Also talked about the need to redefine the relationship between the teacher, the students and the technology.


Oz, S. (2010). Using augmented reality & video games at your classroom. Tel Aviv: Mofet institute.

how will augmented reality change our lives?

A colleague game developer, who teaches game development in high-schools, invited me to judge at the final presentation of her students. In addition, she asked me to talk about augmented reality as the opening keynote for the event.

The audience included both the students, parents and fellow teachers, so I talked about three key points of how augmented reality will influence our future. The first is how things would look like; second is performing simple tasks; and lastly simulate how things behave or act.


Oz, S. (2012). How will augmented reality change our life? Rishon Lezion.

games as a tool to change social behavior

Video games offer genuine engagement and immersive experience for the player. This can be used to change social behavior. The Israeli branch of the Joint Distribution Committee has asked me to create a booth showcasing how games can do that. At the booth I showed: Darfur is Dying, Game for Cancer, Quandry, Spent, Where is the money? (Hebrew), SMS drive (Hebrew), Toilet Trek and Nation States.


Oz, S. (2014). Games as a tool for social behavior. In M. Levy and R. Bar (eds.), New technologies and their evaluation in online teaching and learning. Ma'ale' Hahamisha: Sakoya.
