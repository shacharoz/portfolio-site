Disco Hands at Makers Faire

Disco Hands presents a complex experience of sound, visual and motion, when it’s not clear what leads. The application tracks your fingers, and the more they move, the more the surreal the world evolves to. An endless circle of motion and experience is created. After a short while, the users connect with the music. Then you feel that not only your fingers move, but also other body parts…

Level design best practices: not just for games

Reaching meaningful experience for the user is the holy grail of any product, not just in the gaming or mobile apps. Music, theater, cinema, school, banks, supermarkets, commercials and even architecture. All are speaking in terms of positive experience for the end user. Several theories such as the Flow theory, are trying to explain how to create engagement. But there is still unexplained gap between theory and practice.

Games are highly complex medium, mainly due to the high control the user has on their experience. The level designer is responsible to take the game concept and create that endured engagement. I believe I have discovered the math behind the level design. I also believe that theory can help explain how to create engagement in other fields as well.
